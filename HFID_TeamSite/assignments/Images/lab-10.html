Lab 10

Note: these problems are a little more challenging than most of the ones we have worked on so far.  In particular, you need to make more design decisions, which means there are more chances to make bad decisions.

You are welcome to talk to other students about these design decisions, but don't spoil your fun.  To get the most out of these exercises, you should spend some time thinking and experimenting before you look for help.

1) Do Exercise 12.4 (anagrams).

2) Do Exercise 12.6 (reducible).

3) [Optional] Write a version of ack (Ackermann's function) that uses a memo dictionary to store previously-computed values.
